What Is a Fraction?

A fraction represents part of a whole.

🍕 If a pizza is cut into 4 equal slices, each slice is 1/4.

Ask Zoey:

  • "What does the top number (numerator) tell us?"
  • "What does the bottom number (denominator) tell us?"

Unit Fractions

A unit fraction has a numerator of 1.

Examples: 1/2, 1/3, 1/4, 1/8

Ask Zoey:

  • "Which is bigger: 1/2 or 1/4?"
  • "Why does a bigger denominator mean smaller pieces?"

Fraction Models

We can show fractions using shapes.

🟪 A circle split into equal parts, with some parts shaded, shows a fraction visually.
  • Count the total equal parts — that's the denominator
  • Count the shaded parts — that's the numerator

Ask Zoey: "If 3 out of 5 equal parts are shaded, what fraction is that?"

Comparing Unit Fractions

When the numerator is 1, a bigger denominator means smaller pieces.

1/3 is bigger than 1/8, because the whole is split into fewer, larger pieces.

Ask Zoey: "Would you rather have 1/2 of a candy bar or 1/6 of one? Why?"

Fractions on a Number Line

A number line from 0 to 1 can be split into equal parts to show fractions.

0 ──┬──┬──┬── 1
  1/4  2/4  3/4
  • The line is split into equal jumps
  • Each jump represents one unit fraction

Ask Zoey: "Where would 2/4 land on this number line?"
