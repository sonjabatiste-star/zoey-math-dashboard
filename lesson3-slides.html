Place Value Review

Each digit has a value based on its place.

Example: 4,582
  • Thousands → 4
  • Hundreds → 5
  • Tens → 8
  • Ones → 2

Ask Zoey:

  • “Which digit is in the hundreds place?”
  • “What is the value of the 5?”

Expanded Form

Expanded form shows the value of each digit.

4,582 = 4,000 + 500 + 80 + 2

Ask Zoey:

  • “Can you expand 3,406?”

Rounding Rules

To round, look at the digit to the right of the place you're rounding to.

If it’s 5 or more → round up
If it’s 4 or less → round down

Ask Zoey:

  • “What number tells us whether to round up or down?”

Round to Nearest 10

Example: 47

  • Tens place = 4
  • Ones place = 7 → round up
47 rounds to 50

Ask Zoey:

  • “Does 32 round to 30 or 40?”

Round to Nearest 100

Example: 368

  • Hundreds place = 3
  • Tens place = 6 → round up
368 rounds to 400

Ask Zoey:

  • “Does 241 round to 200 or 300?”

Number Line Rounding

Number lines help us see which ten or hundred a number is closest to.

47 is closer to 50 than 40

Ask Zoey:

  • “Which number is 368 closer to: 300 or 400?”
